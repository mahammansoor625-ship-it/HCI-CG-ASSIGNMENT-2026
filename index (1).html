What is HCI?

Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) focuses on how people interact with computers and how systems can be designed to be intuitive, efficient, and accessible for users.

Chunking

Definition:
Chunking is the grouping of information into smaller, meaningful units to improve memory and understanding.

Role in HCI:

  • Improves recall
  • Reduces cognitive load

Examples:

  • Phone number: 0301-123-4567
  • Menus divided into categories

Short-Term Memory (STM) in HCI

Definition:
Short-term memory temporarily holds information currently in use.

  • Capacity: 5–9 items
  • Duration: 15–30 seconds
  • Easily overloaded

Design Implication:
Interfaces should minimize memory load by keeping information visible and providing feedback.

Fitts’s Law

Definition:
Fitts’s Law predicts the time required to move to a target.

  • Larger targets are easier to select
  • Closer targets require less time

Example:
A large, nearby “Submit” button is faster to click than a small one far away.
